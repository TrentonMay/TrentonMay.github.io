Search Criteria

  • Target Audience

    With this element the target audience is people who are looking for antique items for a collection or whatever their purpose may be. Specifically this element with the images that they used is going to target people looking for antique furniture, guns and clocks. The age group that this would speak to is people who are 35+ years old considering that the majority of younger people aren't very interested in antiques

  • Client Goals and User Needs

    This element speaks to the client goals entirely because of its basic and simple construction which allows the user to clearly interpret what they need to do to operate this feature. This is supported by the fact that it is clear what each section leads to and it conveys what the user needs to click to get to browse the specific items. So if a user wanted to look at antique clocks they can very clearly see what they need to click to browse that topic, which would be the antique clocks square. Additionally there are features in place that tell the user this is a clickable element that allows them to search. What I'm talking about is the green arrow that points to the right. This is powerful reinforcement needed to help users of all skill levels make the conscious decision to click on this element to navigate where they need to go. This in turn allows the client to generate good customer service and may potentially lead to profits because the user will be able to find what they want to buy. This can speak to existing customers/users or even new ones who are just looking for a good antique find.

  • Design Critique

    When it comes to this composition I do really enjoy the design of it all aside from a grievance or two I think that this is a solid piece of work. Starting with the color I do think that the scheme works and it is a good direction to go in. The colors contrast well and it does give you that antique old and classy feel. As for the placement of the elements the designer did a great job at incorporating the users needs into the design. First of all the green button with the white arrow will make the user feel safe in their decision that this is in fact the correct place to click to go to where they need to go.

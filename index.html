When I first started my college journey I started out at Full Sail Online in the MBPS course hoping to one day be a music producer. After almost 2 years in that course I decided that it wasn't the best Idea to go into the music industry due to the fact that it is incredibly difficult to get a job in that field without first having years of experience. While in the process of finding out what I wanted to do instead of music production a friend of mine had introduced me to coding and told me about the fruits of his labor as a freelancer and I was immediately hooked on the idea of being a web developer/designer. Upon this realization of what I want to do with my life I began the apparently long process of switching degrees at FSO. After being out of classes for 5 months while the transfer was taking place I was finally in the classes that I needed to be in to learn how to code and design. There are many challenges that I have faced at FSO and many more I will face in the future but with a little over a year of classes left I am feeling very optimistic that I will graduate and get my dream job.

  • Client Goals and User Needs

    This element speaks to the client goals entirely because of its basic and simple construction which allows the user to clearly interpret what they need to do to operate this feature. This is supported by the fact that it is clear what each section leads to and it conveys what the user needs to click to get to browse the specific items. Additionally there are features in place that tell the user this is a clickable element that allows them to search. What I'm talking about is the green arrow that points to the right. This is powerful reinforcement needed to help users of all skill levels make the concious decision to click on this element to navigate where they need to go

  • Design Critique

    Looking at the design critique I do not completely agree with how it it is featured. First of all I think that just 3 elements is not enough for a page to give a user all of the options that they want to have to search for. Creating only 3 areas for you to search for something ends up requiring them to be displayed larger than what they would be if there were more options which makes them unnecessarily bulky on the page. When it comes to the color I do like the scheme but I feel as if the particular green doesn't portray the mood correctly. The green that is used has a mood that is described as "Guady, Trendy, Tacky, and Sickening." These adjectives do not really portray what this design needs. Possibly "tacky" may work for this design since it is antiques but the overall mood doesn't feel right and I believe that the designer was just looking for well contrasting colors, which he did do right (applause to the designer). A better color to choose for the button would be "Teal Blue", "Silver", or "Tuape" because all three of these colors portray a feeling of "Classic" or "Classy" which suits this perfectly.

  • Design Critique

    When it comes down to it I love this design and I feel like it can be implemented in many ways not just as a welcome message (I'm not too sure why a welcome area would even need a loading bar though) but, I digress. What I love about this design is the simple construction that it takes on, just like they always say on design websites, "KISS", Keep It Simple Stupid! Aside from the overall structure and the simplicity of the elements I think that the color scheme is very well thought out. It makes use of a certain blue that conveys feelings of peacefulness and quiet which is what you want when you are being welcomed to a page. The welcome is never extreme or in your face it's always easing and reassuring that you are in a great place. One pain point that I do have is that the text under the "Welcome" is way too small and will be hard to read for those that have bad vision. Of course the user can always go into their browser settings and change how big the text is displayed on the screen but I think the point of a website is for it to be usable and to not have to make the user do any extra work just to be able to use the product

  • Design Critique

    I for one love this design aside from a few critiques that I have for the UI. One of these critiques is the placement of the search button which covers up some of the photo that visually shows what the user will end up going to. This critique of course can be completely unnecessary if the designer meant for the search icon to only show when the element is hovered upon, but for what is shown I think the search icon shouldn't be where it is. As for the color scheme I think that if the designer is going to use different colors he should use colors that convey the general colors of each food group. A good example of this would be that for fish use blue, for red meat use red, for vegetables use green, bread use brown, grocery/frozen use blue or silver, and the drinks are okay the way that they are. Overall the colors contrast well except for the white letters on the yellow background on the vegetables. The structure of the design is great in that it enables them to have enough options for a user to make a clear choice but it does not crowd the page with too many elements to confuse the user.
